1845

January 29
  • Edgar Allen Poe's "The Raven" is first published (New York Evening Mirror). [1] [5]
February 15
  • William Parsons, Earl of Rosse, first uses 72-inch (183 cm) reflector. [1]
March 1
  • US President John Tyler signs a resolution of Congress to accept Texas as a state. [1] [549.56]
March 3
  • US Congress authorizes ocean mail contracts for foreign mail delivery. [1]
  • Florida becomes 27th state of the USA. [1] [345.9] [453.70]
March 4
  • James K Polk inaugrated as 11th President of the United States. In his inaugural address, Polk states that the US claim to "the country of the Oregon" is "clear and unquestionable". [1] [66.19] [549.50]
March 11
  • British citizen Henry Jones invents self-raising flour. [5] (March 17 [1])
March 17
  • Rubber band patented by Stephen Perry of London, England. [1] [55.53]
March 26
  • Patent awarded for adhesive medicated plaster, precusor of bandaid. [1]
March 28
  • Mexico drops diplomatic relations with US. [1]
April 2
  • H L Fizeau and J Leon Foucault take first photo of Sun. [1]
April 10
  • More than 1,000 buildings are damaged by fire in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, USA. [1]
May 3
  • Fire kills 1,600 in popular theater in Canton, China. [1]
May 6
  • In Ch'ang-shou, Szechwan, China, a stone meteorite damages more than 100 tombs. [521]
May 14
  • Utrecht-Arnhem Railway opens. [1]
May 20
  • First legislative assembly convenes in Hawaii. [1]
May 28
  • Fire in Québec; 1,500 houses destroyed. [1]
June 1
  • Homing pigeon completes 11,000km trip (Namibia-London) in 55 days. [1]
July 4
  • Texas Congress votes for annexation to US. [1]
December 29
  • US President James Polk signs act admitting Texas into union. [1] [549.56] [1075.40]

1846

January 1
  • Yucatan declares independence from México. [1]
January 28
  • Battle of Allwal, British beat Sikhs in Punjab (India). [1]
February 10
  • British defeat Sikhs in battle of Sobraon, India. [1]
February 19
  • Texas is admitted to the United States of America. [1] [400.94]
February 20
  • British occupy Sikh citadel of Lahore. [1]
February 23
  • Polish revolutionaries march on Cracow, but are defeated. [1]
April 18
  • R.E. House of New York City receives a US patent for the telegraph ticker. [457]
April 25
  • Mexican troops kill and wound American soldiers on patrol near Rio Grande. [549.56]
May 3
  • Mexican army surrounds fort in Texas. [1]
May 8
  • First major battle of Mexican War fought at Palo Alto, Texas. American brigadier general Zachary Talyor's army defeats a much larger Mexican force. [1] [573.84]
May 9
  • American brigadier general Zachary Talyor's army defeats a Mexican force at Reseca de la Palma. [1] [573.84]
May 13
  • US declares war on México, two months after fighting began. [1] [549.56]
May 18
  • US troops attack Rio Grande, occupying Matamoros. [1]
May 24
  • General Zachary Taylor captures Monterrey in Mexican War. [1]
June 10
  • Robert Thomson obtains an English patent on a rubber tire. [1]
June 14
  • California (Bear Flag) Republic proclaimed in Sonoma. [1]
June 15
  • The dispute between the US and Great Britain over the Oregon country is settled by treaty, establishing the boundary at the 49th parallel. [1] [549.56]
June 19
  • In Hoboken, New Jersey, USA, the first baseball game under recognizable modern rules is played (Cartwright Rules); New York Nines 23, Knickerbockers 1. [1] [5] [817.142]
June 22
  • Adolphe Sax of Belgium patents his invention of the saxophone. [1] [457]
July 7
  • US annexes California. [1]
July 9
  • Captain John B. Montgomery claims California area for the USA. [1] [1119.105]
July 21
  • Mormons found first English settlement in California (San Joaquin Valley). [1]
August 14
  • The Cape Girardeau meteorite, a 2.3kg chondrite-type meteorite, strikes near the town of Cape Girardeau, Missouri, USA. [5]
August 17
  • US takes Los Angeles. [1]
August 18
  • General Stephen W Kearney's US forces captures Santa Fe, New Mexico. [1]
August 22
  • US annexes New Mexico. [1]
September 10
  • Elias Howe patents the sewing machine. [1] [5]
October 10
  • Queen Isabella of Spain marries first cousin Francisco de Asis de Bourbon. [1157.91]
October 16
  • Dentist William Thomas Green Morton publicly demonstrates the effectiveness of ether. [1]
December 5
  • C F Schoenbein obtains patent for cellulose nitrate explosive. [1]
December 12
  • American chargé d'affaires Benjamin Alden Bidleck signs a treaty with the government of President Tomás Cipriano de Mosquera of New Grenada, which in part guarantees to the US exclusive right of transit across the Isthmus of Panama. [722.32]
December 28
  • Iowa admitted as 29th state of the USA. [1]

1847

January 14
  • Conspiracy in New Mexico against US. [1]
January 24
  • 1,500 New Mexican Indians and Mexicans defeated by US Colonel Price. [1]
February 23
  • Battle of Buena Vista, México; American major general Zachary Taylor defeats Mexican General Antonio Lopez de Santa Anna and Mexican army. [1] [573.84]
February 28
  • US defeats México in battle of Sacramento. [1]
March 1
  • Michigan becomes first English-speaking jurisdiction to abolish the death penalty (except for treason against the state). [1]
March 7
  • US General Scott occupies Vera Cruz, Mexico. [1]
March 18
  • First Dutch public telegram. [1]
March 21
  • Antonio López de Santa Anna becomes President (9th time) of Mexico. [118.61]
March 25
  • Pope Pius IX issues encyclical On aid for Ireland. [1]
March 29
  • 12,000 US troops capture Vera Cruz, Mexico. [1]
April 2
  • Antonio López de Santa Anna resigns (9th time) as President of Mexico. [118.61]
April 14
  • Persia and Osmaanse sign second Treaty of Erzurum. [1]
May 8
  • Robert Thompson patents rubber tire. [1]
May 20
  • Antonio López de Santa Anna becomes President (10th time) of Mexico. [118.61]
May 31
  • Rotterdam-Hague Railway opens. [1]
June 1
  • The first Communist political party, the Communist League, convenes in London, England. [55.43]
July 1
  • First US postage stamps go on sale in New York City: 5-cent Benjamin Franklin and 10-cent George Washington. Franklin is the first person in the world other than a head of state to appear on a stamp. [1] [323.17] [700.2] [1135]
July 14
  • In Braunau, Bohemia, a 37-pound iron meteorite smashes through the roof of a house. [521]
July 24
  • Rotary-type printing press patented by Richard March Hoe, New York City. [1]
July 26
  • Liberia declares independence from American Colonization Society. [1] [1266.60]
August 7
  • First railway in Switzerland opens, the Spanish Bun Railway between Zurich and Baden. [1101.595]
September 14
  • US troops under General Scott enter Mexico City. [1]
September 15
  • Antonio López de Santa Anna resigns (10th time) as President of Mexico. [118.61]
October 1
  • Siemens AG is founded. [5]
November 21
  • Steamer Phoenix is lost on Lake Michigan, 200 dead. [1]
End of 1845-1847. Next: 1848.
