1929

January 2
  • US and Canada agree to preserve Niagara Falls. [1]
January 5
  • Coup by King Alexander in South Slavia. [1]
January 6
  • King Alexander of the Kingdom of Serbs, Croats and Slovenes introduces a personal dictatorship, and decrees a change of name of the country to Kingdom of Yugoslavia. [1268.85]
January 7
  • Tarzan, one of the first adventure comic strips, first appears. [1]
  • First telephone connection between Netherlands and East Indies. [1]
January 8
  • First telephone connection between Netherlands and West-Indies. [1]
January 11
  • Julio Antonio Mella, Cuban revolutionary, murdered at about age 28. [1]
January 12
  • Seatrain (railroad cars on ships) service begins, New Orleans, Louisiana to Havana, Cuba. [1]
January 14
  • Afghan King Amanullah is forced to resign. [1]
January 17
  • Popeye makes first appearance, in comic strip Thimble Theatre. [1]
January 18
  • Josef Stalin proposes to ban Leon Trotsky from the Politburo. [1]
January 20
  • First feature talking motion picture taken outdoors, In Old Arizona. [1]
January 26
  • Indian National Congress proclaims goal for India's independence. [1]
January 31
  • Leon Trotsky expelled from Russia to Turkey. [1]
February 3
  • Revolutionary Socialist Party forms in Amsterdam, Netherlands. [1]
February 9
  • A Protocol (Litvinov Pact) renouncing war as an instrument of national policy is signed by the Soviet Union, Estonia, Latvia, Poland, and Romania. [10]
February 11
  • The Latern Treaty creates the Vatican (world's smallest country), giving sovereignty of the Vatican to the Holy See. [1] [408.109]
February 14
  • Saint Valentine's Day Massacre in Chicago, Illinois, USA; seven gangsters killed. [1]
February 19
  • Medical diathermy machine first used, in Schenectady, New York. [1]
February 20
  • American Samoa organized as a territory of US. [1]
February 26
  • The Grand Tetons in Wyoming is established as a national park in the United States. [1] [5] [129]
February 27
  • Russia and US sign trade agreement. [1]
  • Turkey signs Litvinov-pact. [1]
March 4
  • Herbert Hoover inaugurated as 31st US President. [1]
March 6
  • Turkey and Bulgaria sign friendship treaty. [1]
March 8
  • US worker union commission reports of slavery in Liberia. [1]
March 17
  • General Motors acquires German auto manufacturer Adam Opel. [1]
  • Spanish dictator Primo de Rivera closes university of Madrid. [1]
March 22
  • US Coast Guard vessel sinks Canadian schooner suspected of carrying liquor. [1]
March 28
  • Democratic constitution goes into effect in Ecuador. [1]
April 1
  • Austrian government of Ignaz Seipel falls. [1]
April 3
  • Persia agrees to Litvinov Pact. [1]
April 5
  • Lithuania signs Litvinov-pact. [1]
April 11
  • Loetafoon celluloid film system demonstrated in Amsterdam. [1]
April 18
  • Palace for People's industry in Amsterdam devastated by fire. [1]
April 24
  • First non-stop England-to-India flight takes-off. [1]
  • Thorvald Stauning becomes premier of Denmark. [1]
April 26
  • First non-stop England to India flight lands. [1]
April 30
  • Earnest Streeruwitz becomes chancellor of Austria. [1]
May 1
  • Farm workers' strike begins in East-Groningen. [1]
  • Police kill 19 Mayday demonstrators in Berlin, Germany. [1]
May 3
  • Prussia bans anti-fascists. [1]
May 8
  • Jan Mayen island, 500 km NNE of Iceland, is incorporated into Norway. [1]
May 12
  • Pulitzer prize awarded to Julia Peterkin (Scarlet Sister Mary). [1]
May 15
  • Fire in X-ray film stock kills 125 at Crile Clinic (Cleveland, Ohio, USA). [1]
May 19
  • General Feng Yu-Xiang of China declares war on Chiang Kai-Shek Government. [1]
June 3
  • Border dispute between Peru and Chile resolved. [1]
June 7
  • Vatican City becomes a soverign state. [1]
June 27
  • First color TV demonstration (New York City). [1]
June 29
  • First high-speed jet wind tunnel completed, at Langley Field, California, USA. [1]
July
  • Maiden voyage of North German Lloyd's Bremen to New York completed in record time: 4 days, 16 hours, 42 minutes. [260.48] [274.13]
July 24
  • The Treaty of Paris becomes effective, with 31 more countries signing compliance. [10]
  • President Herbert Hoover proclaims Kellogg-Briand Pact which renounces war. [1]
August 8
  • German airship Graf Zeppelin begins a round-the-world flight from Lakehurst, New Jersey, USA. [1]
August 29
  • German airship Graf Zeppelin arrives back at Lakehurst, New Jersey, USA, completing a round-the-world flight. [1]
September 3
  • The Dow Jones Industrial Average hits a high point of 381.17. (This pre-crash high is not surpassed until 1954, adjusted for inflation.) [227]
September 25
  • Lieutenant Jimmy Doolittle performs the first blind flight at Mitchell Field in New York, proving that full Instrument Flying from take off to landing is possible. [5] (September 24 [1])
September 30
  • First manned rocket plane flight (by auto maker Fritz von Opel). [1]
October 3
  • Kingdom of Serbs, Croats and Slovenes officially changes name to Yugoslavia. [1286]
October 23
  • The first North American transcontinental air service begins between New York City and Los Angeles, California. [5]
October 24
  • First day of New York stock market crash, Black Thursday. [1] [555.30]
October 28
  • First child born in aircraft, in Miami, Florida, USA. [1]
  • New York Stock Enchange drops 12.9 percent. [905.26]
October 29
  • "Black Tuesday" New York stock market crashes, triggers "Great Depression". [1] [450.52]
November 18
  • Large earthquake in Atlantic breaks Transatlantic cable in 28 places. [1]
December 1
  • Game of BINGO invented by Edwin S Lowe. [1]
December 2
  • First skull of Peking man found, 50km out of Peking at Tsjoe Koe Tien. [1]
December 6
  • Turkey introduces female suffrage. [1]
December 8
  • In Zvezvan, Yugoslavia, a falling meteorite kills one person in a bridal party. [521]
December 14
  • Alexander Zaimis elected President of Greece. [1]
December 29
  • Indonesian police arrest Sukarno and 100s PNI-leaders. [1]
December 31
  • Pope Pius XI publishes encyclical Divini illius magistri. [1]
End of 1929. Next: 1930.
