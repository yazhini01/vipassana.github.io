1949

January 1
  • Tokelau (Union) Islands declared part of New Zealand. [1]
January 5
  • General Spoor orders cease-fire on Sumatra. [1]
January 7
  • First photo of genes taken at University of Southern California by Pease and Baker. [1]
January 10
  • RCA introduces the 7-inch 45rpm vinyl record, and Columbia introduces the 12-inch 33.3rpm record. [457]
January 12
  • Dutch court affirms death sentence against SS chief Hanns Rauter. [1]
January 14
  • Black/Indian race rebellion in Durban, South Africa; 142 die. [1]
January 15
  • Mao's Red army conquers Ten-tsin. [1]
January 25
  • First Israeli election - Ben-Gurion's Mapai party wins. [1]
January 27
  • Chinese liner Taiping collides with a collier off south China. [1]
January 28
  • United Nations Security council condemns Dutch aggression in Indonesia. [1]
January 29
  • Britain, Belgium, Luxembourg, Netherlands, New Zealand and Switzerland recognize Israel. [1]
February 1
  • 200-inch (5.08-metre) Hale telescope first used. [1]
  • RCA releases first single record ever (45 RPM). [1]
February 4
  • Failed assassination attempt on Shah of Persia. [1]
February 12
  • Panic in Quito, Ecuador, after War of the Worlds is played on radio. [1]
  • Unidentified aircraft bombs Jerusalem. [1]
February 14
  • First session of Knesset opens in Jerusalem, Israel. [1]
  • Dutch Drees government presents plan for the building of 30,000 houses. [1]
February 17
  • Chaim Weizman declared President of Israel. [1]
February 19
  • First Bollingen Prize for poetry awarded to Ezra Pound. [1]
  • Mass arrests of communists in India. [1]
February 20
  • First International Pancake Race held (Liberal, Kansas, USA). [1]
February 24
  • Israel and Egypt sign an armistice agreement. [1]
  • V-2/WAC-Corporal first rocket to outer space, White Sands, New Mexico, 400km. [1]
February 26
  • US Air Force plane begins first nonstop around-the-world flight. [1]
March 2
  • First automatic street light (New Milford, Connecticut, USA). [1]
  • Lucky Lady II (US Air Force B-50 Superfortress) completes first nonstop round-the-world flight at Fort Worth Texas, covering 23,452-miles in 94 hours. [1]
March 4
  • Andrei Vishinsky succeeds Molotov as Soviet Foreign minister. [1]
  • Piet Van de Pol (Netherlands) becomes world champion billiard player. [1]
  • Security Council of United Nations recommends membership for Israel. [1]
March 18
  • NATO (North Atlantic Treaty Organization) pact ratified. [1]
March 25
  • Hanns A Rauter, German SS-commandant in Netherlands, executed at age 54. [1]
March 29
  • Turkey recognizes Israel. [1]
March 31
  • Newfoundland becomes Canada's 10th province. [1]
April 4
  • Israel and Jordan sign armistice agreement. [1]
  • North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) pact signed in Washington DC by US, Britain, France and Canada. [1] [614.23]
April 5
  • 60-year-old Saint Anthony's Hospital burns, kills 77 (Effingham, Illinois, USA). [1]
April 6
  • Israel releases its first coins into circulation; the first is a 25-mil coin of aluminum-manganese. [407.18]
April 7
  • Rogers and Hammerstein's play South Pacific opens at Majestic Theater in New York (for 1928 performances). [1]
April 9
  • United Nations' International Court of Justice holds Albania responsible for incidents in Corfu Channel and awards Britain damages. [1]
April 15
  • Pope Pius XII publishes encyclical Redemptoris nostri. [1]
April 16
  • Sutan Ibrahim gelor Datuk Tan Malaka Indonesia, communist, executed at age 54. [1]
April 18
  • Republic of Ireland withdraws from British Commonwealth. [1]
April 23
  • Chinese Red army conquers Nanjing. [1]
  • Netherlands annexes Elten and Tudderen. [1]
May 2
  • Bolivian state of siege proclaimed. [1]
May 4
  • Air crash at Turijn (whole Torino-soccer team survives). [1]
May 5
  • In London, England, the Statute of the Council of Europe is signed by representatives of ten countries. The Council is set up to create greater unity in Europe. [7]
May 8
  • West German constitution approved. [1]
May 9
  • Prince Louis II of Monaco dies. [1]
  • Prince Rainier III becomes leader of Monaco. [1]
May 11
  • First Polaroid camera sells for US$89.95 (New York City). [1]
  • By a vote of 37-12, Israel becomes 59th member of United Nations. [1]
  • Siam again renames itself Thailand. [1]
May 12
  • Soviet land blockade of Berlin ends. [1] [988.646]
  • The Israeli flag is first unfurled at the United Nations building in New York. [406.76]
May 13
  • First British-produced jet bomber, Canberra, makes its first test flight. [1]
May 17
  • British government recognizes Republic of Ireland. [1]
May 18
  • James T Adams; US historian (Pulitzer 1921). [1]
May 23
  • Federal Republic of [West] Germany proclaimed. [1]
May 25
  • Chinese Red army occupies Shanghai. [1]
May 30
  • German Democratic Republic constitution approved. [1]
  • NPS/VHP win first general election in Suriname. [1]
June 2
  • Transjordan is renamed Jordan. [1]
June 14
  • State of Vietnam formed. [1]
June 15
  • Taiwan replaces old dollar with new dollar at rate of one new dollar for 40,000 old dollars. [439.38]
June 16
  • Gas turbine-electric locomotive demonstrated, Erie, Pennsylvania, USA. [1]
June 29
  • South Africa begins implementing apartheid; no mixed marriages. [1]
  • US troops withdraw from Korea after WW II. [1]
July 1
  • Bao Dai's Republic of Vietnam gains independence from France. [1]
July 10
  • First practical rectangular TV tube announced in Toledo, Ohio. Size is 12 by 16 inches, price is US$12. [1] [457]
July 15
  • Czech tennis stars Jaroslav Drobny and Vladimir Cernik defect to US. [1]
July 19
  • Laos becomes associated state within French Union. [1]
July 20
  • Israel's 19-month war of independence ends. [1]
July 21
  • US Senate ratifies North Atlantic Treaty by a vote of 82-13. [1]
July 27
  • The de Havilland Comet, the first jet-powered airliner, makes its initial flight. [1] [5]
August 8
  • Bhutan becomes an independent monarchy. [1]
August 10
  • First successful flight of Avro Jetliner in Canada, first passenger jet aircraft in North America. [1178.400]
August 20
  • Hungary (Magyar People's Republic) accepts constitution. [1]
August 24
  • North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) established. [1] [960.26]
August 29
  • USSR explodes its first atomic bomb. [1]
September 2
  • Fire in riverfront area kills 1,700 (Chungking, China). [1]
September 4
  • The Bristol Brabazon makes its maiden flight. [5]
September 17
  • Steamer Noronic burns at pier killing 128 (Toronto, Canada). [1]
September 21
  • Control of Western Germany passes from the Allied Military Government to a supervised Federal Republic of Germany democracy. [1] [10]
  • In Beddgelert, Wales, a meteorite strikes a building. [521]
September 23
  • US President Harry Truman announces evidence of USSR's first nuclear device detonation. [1]
September 30
  • Berlin Airlift ends after 277,000 flights. [1]
October 1
  • Chairman Mao Tse-tung proclaims the founding of the People's Republic of China. [1] [5] [409.22] [557.50]
  • Republic of China (Taiwan) forms on the island of Formosa. [1]
  • The US suspends diplomatic relations with China. [557.50]
October 6
  • Iva Toguri D'Aquino (Japanese wartime propaganda broadcaster Tokyo Rose) sentenced to ten years and $10,000 fine. [1] [457]
  • US President Harry Truman signs Mutual Defense Assistance Act (for NATO). [1]
  • The Allies end the Berlin airlift. [988.646]
October 7
  • German Democratic Republic formed from Russian occupation zone (National Day). [1]
October 15
  • Administration of territory of Manipur taken over by Indian government. [1]
  • Tripura accedes to the Indian union. [1]
October 22
  • 200 killed in train derailment near Nowy Dwor, Poland. [1]
November 19
  • Prince Rainier III coronation in Monaco. [1]
November 20
  • In Kochi, Japan, a hot meteoritic stone enters a house through a window. [521]
November 24
  • Britain nationalizes its steel and iron industry. [1]
November 26
  • India adopts a constitution as a British Commonwealth Republic. [1]
November 30
  • Chinese Communists capture Chungking. [1]
December 7
  • Chiang Kai-shek evacuates Chinese government to island of Formosa. [1] [916.103]
December 9
  • Dutch second Chamber accepts Indonesian sovereignty. [1]
December 13
  • Knesset votes to transfer Israel's capitol to Jerusalem. [1]
December 16
  • Sukarno becomes President of Indonesia, with Mokammed Hatta as premier. [1]
  • Traitsjo Kostov, Bulgarian communist vice-premier, is executed. [1]
December 17
  • Burma recognizes People's Republic of China. [1]
December 21
  • Dutch first Chamber accepts sovereignty of Indonesia. [1]
December 27
  • Queen Juliana (Netherlands) grants sovereignty to the Federated Republik of Indonesia, ending its War of Independence. [1] [542.125] [967.617] [1153.1032]
December 29
  • First UHF television station operating regular basis (Bridgeport, Connecticut, USA). [1]
  • Hungary nationalizes its industries. [1]
December 30
  • India recognizes People's Republic of China. [1]
December 31
  • Eighteen countries recognize Republic of Indonesia. [1]
