1970

January 1
  • Neth Christian Workers Union (NCW) forms. [1]
  • Revised calendar for Western (Roman Catholic) Church goes into effect. [1]
January 3
  • Marxist government takes over in Congo. [1]
  • The Prairie Network of cameras on parairie states of the USA records the fall of a stony meteorite, with four fragments totalling 17kg recovered near Lost City in Oklahoma, Nebraska. [523.46]
January 4
  • In Tonghai, Yunnan Province, China, a magnitude 7.5 earthquake occurs. 10,000 deaths. The quake was felt at Hanoi, Vietnam. [53]
January 5
  • 23,000 Belgian mine workers strike. [1]
January 9
  • Constitution of Singapore enacted. [1]
January 12
  • Biafran War ends, Biafra surrenders to Nigeria. [1]
  • Boeing 747 makes its maiden voyage. [1]
January 15
  • Republic of Biafra disbands, re-joins Nigeria. [1] [686.15]
January 16
  • Colonel Kadhaffi becomes premier of Libya. [1]
January 19
  • Dutch bishop says he is in favor of married priest. [1]
January 20
  • In Hokkaido, Japan, a magnitude 6.7 earthquake occurs. One person killed, 38 injured. [53]
January 22
  • First commercial Boeing 747 flight (Pan Am), New York to London in 6.5 hours. [1]
January 23
  • Australia's first amateur radio satellite (Oscar 5) launched (from California). [1]
  • US launches second generation weather satellite, ITOS 1. [1]
January 27
  • American movie rating system modifies "M" rating to "PG". [1]
January 28
  • Lubomír Strougal succeeds Cernik as premier of Czechoslovakia. [1]
January 29
  • USSR performs nuclear test at Eastern Kazakstan/Semipalatinsk. [1]
February 1
  • Stalled commuter train rammed by express in Argentina, 139 die. [1]
  • Federal Republic of Germany and USSR sign gas contract. [1]
February 5
  • In central Philippines (Romblon Island), a magnitude 6.6 earthquake occurs. Widespread damage and three deaths. [53]
February 10
  • Dry powder avalanche moving at 120 mph smashes into youth hostel killing 40 Belgian, French, and German youths (Val d'Isere, France). [1]
February 11
  • Japan becomes fourth nation to put a satellite (Osumi) in orbit. [1]
February 13
  • Man-eating tiger is reported to have killed 48, 80 km from New Delhi, India. [1]
February 15
  • Dominican DC-9 crashes into sea at Santo Domingo, kills 102. [1]
  • Nationalists disrupt United Nations' session on Congo. [1]
February 17
  • The Republic of Rhodesia replaces the pound with the dollar as currency. [699.24]
February 19
  • USSR launches Sputnik 52 and Molniya 1-13 communications satellite. [1]
February 21
  • Pathet Lao conquers Xieng Khuang and Muong Suy in Laos. [1]
  • Swissair Flight 330 to Tel Aviv has a parcel bomb explode in its cargo hold, crashes, killing 38 passengers and 9 crew. [635.74]
February 23
  • Guyana becomes a republic (National Day). [1]
  • Holy Eucharist given by women for first time in Roman Catholic service. [1]
February 24
  • 29 Swiss Army officers die in avalanche (Reckingen, Switzerland). [1]
March 1
  • End of US commercial whale hunting. [1]
  • Kreisky's social-democrats win Austrian parliamentary election. [1]
  • White government of Rhodesia declares independence from Britain. [1]
March 2
  • American Airlines' first flight of a Boeing 747. [1]
March 4
  • French submarine Eurydice explodes. [1]
March 5
  • Nuclear non-proliferation treaty goes into effect. [1]
March 11
  • Iraq Ba'th Party recognizes Kurd nation. [1]
March 13
  • Digital Equipment Corp introduces PDP-11 minicomputer. [1]
March 14
  • In Canada, Quebec is the first province to launch a legal lottery. The Inter Plus lottery offers a CDN$150,000 grand prize for $2 tickets. [40.19]
March 15
  • Expo '70 opens in Osaka, Japan. [1]
March 16
  • New English Bible published. [1]
March 17
  • US casts their first United Nations Security Council veto (Support England). [1]
March 18
  • Cambodia military coup under General Lon Nol; prince Sihanuk flees. [1]
March 19
  • West German Chancellor Willy Brandt and East German Premier Willi Stoph meet. [1]
March 20
  • The oil tanker Othello collides in Tralhavet Bay, Sweden, spilling over 69.6 million litres of oil. [522]
March 23
  • US President Richard Nixon declares a state of national emergency. [861.337]
March 24
  • In New York City, Robert A. Siegel Auction Galleries Inc. Annual Rarity Sale sells the unique British Guiana 1-cent black on magenta postage stamp of 1856 for US$280,000 to Irwin Weinberg, American stamp dealer, a record for a single stamp. [267.255] [674.86] [1156.8] [1170.58] [1268.7]
March 25
  • Concorde makes its first supersonic flight (700 MPH/1,127 KPH). [1]
March 26
  • 500th nuclear explosion announced by the US since 1945. [1]
March 27
  • USSR performs nuclear test at Eastern Kazakhstan/Semipalitinsk. [1]
March 28
  • In Gediz, Turkey, a magnitude 6.9 earthquake occurs. Over 1,000 killed, more than 12,000 houses destroyed or severely damaged in the Gediz-Emet area of Kutahya Province. Over 50 percent of the buildings were damaged in 53 villages in the area. Strong aftershocks cause considerable additional damage. [1] [53]
April 2
  • The 52nd Annual New York Automobile Show is held in New York, USA. Chevrolet unveils the experimental transverse mid-engined XP-882 Corvette prototype. Ford shows its Pantera, and American Motors shows its AMX/3, both mid-engine prototypes. [8]
  • Meghalaya becomes autonomous state within India's Assam state. [1]
  • Qatar gains independence from Britain. [1]
April 7
  • Netherlands issues the world's first postage stamps with images created by computer. [268.341]
  • In Philippine Islands, a magnitude 7.5 earthquake occurs. [53]
April 11
  • Apollo 13, the third manned lunar landing mission, is launched from Cape Canaveral, Florida, carrying astronauts James A. Lovell, John L. Swigert, and Fred W. Haise. [1] [129]
April 13
  • Apollo 13 announces "Houston, we've got a problem!" as Beech-built oxygen tank explodes en route to Moon. [1] [129]
  • Greek composer Mikis Theordorakis is freed. [1]
April 15
  • Libyan leader Qadhafi launches "Green Revolution". [1]
April 16
  • 70 die in an avalanche (France). [1]
April 17
  • Apollo 13 limps back safely to Earth. [1] [5] [129]
April 20
  • Bruno Kreisky becomes first socialist chancellor of Austria. [1]
April 22
  • First Earth Day held internationally to conserve natural resources; largest single protest in American history. [1] [5] [129] [248.45]
April 24
  • Gambia becomes a republic within the Commonwealth. [1]
  • People's Rebublic of China launches its first satellite, Dong Fang Hong I, transmitting song "East is Red". [1] [5]
  • Senegal adopts constitution. [1]
April 29
  • 50,000 US and South Vietnamese troops invade Cambodia. [1]
End of 1970 January-April. Next: 1970 May.
